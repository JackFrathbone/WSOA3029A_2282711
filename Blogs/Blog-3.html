An Analysis of My Own Website Design

After previously looking at another website's design and layout, this week it's my turn to look inwards and critically analyse the design of my own website. While as I'm writing this I don't have a website up yet, so I will be going over my wireframe plan for the site which covers all the design and layout choices I have made so far. So anything I state here is probably subject to change as my site goes live and I adjust parts of my design in response to reality website design over my plans, but overall the structure should mostly be intact.

Firstly, it is important to understand the context of my website. The focus I am going for is on visualizing data on the topic of the game development industry in South Africa, using data from the South African Cultural Observatory. This data talks about the growth, revenue, size and future directions of the game industry in South Africa, and the site will seek to use this data to tell a story about the industry, show how it has grown and the geographical location of South Africans studios. So primarily the site will focus on showing this data, visually, and with accompanied text and images to engage the viewer. It will also house these blogs in a separate section, alongside the usual website requirements like a contact and ‘about us’ page. It will also have a page dedicated to credits for all of the external assets used.

As seen below in image 1 the site will have a very simple navigation bar, basically just a white space with a large distinct font for the website title, with thinner text for the links to each page. These links will become bold and underlined when the mouse goes over them, in order to clearly show they are selected. The overall look of the homepage is focused on visual clarity and minimalism, putting all the focus on the content. The site overall uses a four colour palette for everything(red, yellow, purple and white), with purple being used as a frame for all content, always clearly showing what is the body opr article of the specific page. Both the text and any visuals in the body of the site is intended to have a white colour, or white background to make them clearly stand out from the purple background and highlight them as being the content. All pages will follow a similar layout to the homepage with a purple body and white content, with the heading of the page always being large and in yellow.

Hassenzahl and Tractinsky, when talking about UX, describe it as a design focus that moves beyond functionality to address the experience of pleasurable user experience and the aesthetic qualities of an interface (Hassenzahl & Tractinsky, 2006). Functionally my website is straightforward, it just need to display data on a single page that scrolls down, but to create a positive user experience it is also necessary to consider the pleasure of using the site, with the current idea to have a smooth scroll on the homepage where the various graphs and image will enlarge as you scroll over them, showing text and detail, and then shrinking down when you move on to the next section. Alongside the simple yet colourful visuals this should help emphasize the data and put it and the accompanying text front and centre for the user.
